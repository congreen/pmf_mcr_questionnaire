Generator operation below 30% rated capacity significantly increases maintenance cost.
Generator Load Factor — Underloading (OF1)
Frequent short-duration generator starts (<30 min per activation) cause disproportionate engine wear.
Start Frequency / Short-Cycling (OF2)
Maintenance scheduling discipline (executing visits on time vs. deferring) is a critical driver of maintenance cost.
Maintenance Scheduling Compliance (OF3)
Battery-first dispatch (battery absorbs short outages before generator start) will significantly reduce maintenance cost.
Battery-First Dispatch Strategy (OF4)
On Battery-first dispatch, faulty battery significantly increases generator running hours.
Faulty Battery (OF5)
Generator running hours significantly influence generator maintenance cost more than generator age.
Running Hours vs. Generator Age (OF6)
Operator skill level directly influences maintenance quality at NICTBB sites.
Operator Skill/Competence (OF7)
